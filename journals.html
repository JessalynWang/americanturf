During this week, the first week, we got everything started up. Mr. Vo’s approved our website and we were able to see our newly assigned plots. Our plots of soil is located in the center back side of our box. It has pretty good shading yet it is not covered by trees and gets a good amount of sunlight. We started out this week with planting two tomato plants in a styrofoam nursery. By the end of the week we did not have any sign of growth but maybe we will have some progress next week.

WEEK 2

Previous Next

For week 2 not much had changed. This week we decided to plant the rest of our vegetables in the styrofoam nursery. We also decided to replant the tomatoes that were not growing from the previous week. On friday we were given family time, so we watered all of our plants to make sure they weren't going to dry out, and made sure they received the amount of sunlight they needed to grow. Throughout this week we also went down to the garden to check on out plot of dirt to make sure it had enough water so we would be ready to plant out vegetables next week.

WEEK 3

Previous Next

We yet again replanted our seeds. This marks the third time we have replanted, although this time we decided to use two small separate containers to plant carrots. We watered our plants daily, but as of the end of the week it seems there is minimal change. After discussing on Friday, we agreed that we will give the plants until week 4 to sprout. Afterwards, we will be too pressed for time. Serop and Aramis will go to Home Depot and purchase seedlings should there be no change by week 4.

WEEK 4

Previous Next

This week was our best week so far. Earlier in the week, Serop and Aramis went to Home Depot to get some seedlings to help boost our garden. We purchased two bell pepper seedlings, two tomato seedlings, 3 garlic bulbs, two basil seedlings, and two rosemary seedlings. Aramis and Serop will keep track of one of the two bell pepper seedlings, one of the two tomato seedlings, one of the two basil seedlings and one of the two rosemary seedlings at home. The rest will be grown in our garden. On Friday, we were given family time so we decided to plant a potato that Mr. Vo gave us. We chose not to water the potato because the dirt was already moist and rain was expected for the weekend.

WEEK 5

Previous Next

We’ve got progress! The plants at the garden have grown at a tremendous rate, cilantro taking the lead. The growth rate of plants at home seems to be a tad bit slower than the ones at school, perhaps because the garden soil filled into the plots provides extra nutrients. If this extreme growth continues, we may need to move the herbs to leave space for the larger plants to avoid unnecessary death. At the moment, we are on a good track.

WEEK 6

Previous Next

We are very happy with our progress this week. We had one hiccup on Monday as we found our basil completely dead; leaves were wilted and eaten. We found a few rollie-pollies in our garden so we through those out since they commonly eat crop leaves. We had the great opportunity of receiving some broccoli plugs donated by a student form the previous period. We pulled out the dead basil plant and planted the broccoli plugs in its place. We did not have to do much watering this week as we got a good amount of rain at the end of week 5 and had heavy rain Tuesday-Wednesday. I think our plants should be in good shape next week as they received a good amount of water this week. Hoping for good weather next week!
